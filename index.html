The Ultimate Movie Musical Showdown: Top 5 Picks

Movie musicals bring the magic of Broadway to the big screen! I'm breaking down my top favorites and why each one deserves a standing ovation.

Start Scrolling

1. Wicked

My all-time favorite movie musical right now, no question! This show has absolutely blown me away every time I’ve watched it (which is five times and counting). The scenery, the acting, the vocals — everything is just on another level, giving me chills no matter how many times I see it. And the music? Unmatched. The entire soundtrack has made it into my daily listening rotation, which is rare for me — I usually don’t love every song on a movie soundtrack, but Wicked is just that good. Let’s just say, something has changed within me since watching it, and I wouldn’t have it any other way.

2. Mamma Mia

This movie will forever hold a special place in my heart. It was one of the first movie musicals I ever watched, and when the second one came out, it only deepened my love for this franchise. There’s just something so joyful and freeing about it — it’s the ultimate girlhood experience with its perfect mix of laughter, tears, and those classic ABBA vibes. The scenery is breathtaking, the cast is stacked with talent, and every time I watch, I feel like packing my bags and traveling the world like Donna.

3. The Greatest Showman

There’s something truly magical about The Greatest Showman. The story, visuals, and music work together so seamlessly to create an experience that’s both heart-tugging and inspiring. This movie (and its music) got me through some of my toughest times, and even now, I find myself coming back to the soundtrack over and over again. Let’s be real — I’ve probably listened to "Rewrite the Stars" a million times and still haven’t gotten tired of it. And if that’s not a testament to how good it is, I don’t know what is.

4. Grease

This movie musical is pure nostalgia for me. I’ve watched it countless times with my mom, and it’s been a soundtrack to so many car rides and childhood movie nights. The talent in this cast is undeniable — John Travolta and Olivia Newton-John were meant for these roles. Sure, it’s a throwback, and some parts might feel a little outdated now, but for its time, it was incredible. And honestly? It still holds up as one of the best. Grease will always be a top-tier classic in my book.

5. Chicago

I went into Chicago not knowing what to expect, but the moment I saw a clip of "We Both Reached for the Gun," I was hooked. There’s something so cunning and unique about its musical numbers—it definitely offers a different vibe than the others on my list. The movie’s mix of mystery, wit, and theatrical flair made for such an interesting watch. Admittedly, there were moments where I wasn’t quite as intrigued, but overall, it was a well-done adaptation of the Broadway classic.
